# Email Sequence Generation

Generate complete, ready-to-send email sequences with subject lines, body copy, timing, and segmentation strategies built on proven frameworks and industry benchmarks.

## When to Use

- Creating welcome, nurture, launch, or re-engagement email sequences
- Building cart abandonment recovery flows
- Designing cold outreach sequences for B2B prospecting
- Planning email marketing strategy with segmentation

## Core Email Philosophy

Every email must have exactly ONE primary purpose:
- ONE main idea or story
- ONE call-to-action
- ONE desired reader action

Violating this rule is the number one cause of low click-through rates.

## Sequence Types

| Sequence Type | When to Use | Emails | Goal |
|--------------|-------------|--------|------|
| **Welcome** | New subscriber | 5-7 | Build trust, deliver value, introduce product |
| **Nurture** | Warm leads | 6-8 | Educate, build authority, overcome objections |
| **Launch** | New product/feature | 8-12 | Build anticipation, drive purchases |
| **Re-engagement** | Inactive (30-90 days) | 3-4 | Win back or clean list |
| **Onboarding** | New trial/customer | 5-7 | Drive activation, reduce churn |
| **Cart Abandonment** | Abandoned checkout | 3-4 | Recover lost sales |
| **Cold Outreach** | B2B prospecting | 3-5 | Book meetings |

## Email Structure Frameworks

**Value Before Ask:**
```
Email 1: Pure value (no ask)
Email 2: Pure value (no ask)
Email 3: Value + soft mention of product
Email 4: Value + case study showing results
Email 5: Direct ask with urgency
```
Ratio: approximately 3:1 value-to-ask.

**Story-Driven:**
```
Hook: Story, observation, or surprising fact (2-3 sentences)
Bridge: Connect to the reader's situation (1-2 sentences)
Lesson: Actionable insight (2-3 sentences)
CTA: Link to next step (1 sentence + button/link)
```

**Problem-Agitate-Solution:**
```
Problem: "Are you struggling with [specific pain]?"
Agitate: "Every day you wait, [consequence]..."
Solution: "[Product] solves this by [mechanism]."
CTA: "Start your free trial and see the difference in 24 hours."
```

## Subject Line Formulas

| Formula | Example | Best For |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Number + Benefit | "3 ways to double your conversion rate" | Educational |
| Curiosity Gap | "The pricing mistake that cost me $50K" | Story-driven |
| Direct Benefit | "Your copy report is ready" | Delivery/welcome |
| Personalization | "[Name], your trial expires tomorrow" | Urgency/onboarding |
| Question | "Are you making this SEO mistake?" | Problem-awareness |
| Social Proof | "Why 5,000 marketers switched this month" | Nurture/launch |
| Pattern Interrupt | "I was wrong about email marketing" | Re-engagement |

**Rules:**
- Keep under 50 characters for mobile (40 ideal)
- Front-load important words
- Use numbers (odd outperform even)
- Avoid spam trigger words in excess
- Always write preview text (preheader) alongside subject

## Sequence Templates

### Welcome Sequence (5-7 Emails)

- **Email 1 (Immediate):** Deliver lead magnet + set expectations + ask engaging question
- **Email 2 (Day 1):** Founder/origin story + empathy + shared experience
- **Email 3 (Day 3):** Educational content demonstrating expertise
- **Email 4 (Day 5):** Case study with specific numbers + soft pitch
- **Email 5 (Day 7):** Direct pitch + address top 3 objections + risk reversal
- **Email 6 (Day 10, optional):** Limited-time welcome offer
- **Email 7 (Day 14, optional):** Transition + segment by topic preference

### Cold Outreach (3-5 Emails)

- **Email 1 (Day 1):** 3-4 sentences. Lead with research. Offer specific value.
- **Email 2 (Day 4):** Reference Email 1. Share relevant case study result.
- **Email 3 (Day 8):** Breakup email. Offer no-strings resource.
- **Email 4 (Day 14, optional):** New angle based on trigger event.
- **Email 5 (Day 21, optional):** Graceful final close.

### Cart Abandonment (3-4 Emails)

- **Email 1 (1 hour):** Simple reminder with product image. No discount yet.
- **Email 2 (24 hours):** Address objections + customer testimonial.
- **Email 3 (72 hours):** Time-limited discount with urgency.
- **Email 4 (7 days, optional):** Last chance, cart expiring.

## Send Timing

| Sequence | Day 1 | Day 2 | Day 3 | Day 4 | Day 5 | Day 7+ |
|----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Welcome | Email 1 | Email 2 | -- | Email 3 | -- | Email 4+ |
| Nurture | Email 1 | -- | Email 2 | -- | -- | Every 3-4 days |
| Cart Abandon | 1hr | -- | 24hr | -- | 72hr | -- |

**Best Send Times:**
- B2B: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM recipient time
- B2C: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM or 7-9 PM
- E-commerce: Thursday-Sunday promotional, Tue-Wed educational

## Segmentation Strategies

| Segment Basis | Examples | How to Use |
|--------------|---------|------------|
| Behavior | Page visits, clicks, downloads | Trigger relevant sequences |
| Engagement | Open rate, click rate | Separate engaged vs dormant |
| Source | Organic, paid, referral | Tailor welcome sequence |
| Stage | Lead, trial, customer, churned | Different sequences per stage |
| Value | Purchase amount, plan tier | Prioritize high-value segments |

## Compliance

**CAN-SPAM (US):** Physical address required, clear unsubscribe, accurate sender info
**GDPR (EU):** Explicit opt-in, documented consent, right to deletion
**CASL (Canada):** Express consent required, sender identification

Always recommend verification with legal counsel.

## A/B Testing Hierarchy

1. Subject lines (biggest impact on open rate)
2. CTA and offer (biggest impact on click rate)
3. Send timing
4. Email length and format
