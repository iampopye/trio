# Product Launch Playbook

Generate a complete, week-by-week launch playbook for any product, service, or feature launch with templates, checklists, email sequences, social posts, and metrics tracking.

## When to Use

- Planning a new product, service, or feature launch
- Coordinating a multi-channel launch campaign
- Creating a go-to-market strategy
- Building launch email and social content

## Step 1: Gather Context

Collect before generating:
1. What are you launching?
2. Who is the target audience?
3. Primary launch goal (revenue target, signups, awareness)
4. Launch date
5. Available channels (email list size, social following, ad budget)
6. Price point
7. Existing customers/users for beta or testimonials
8. Budget level

## Step 2: Determine Launch Type

| Launch Type | Best For | Timeline |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Product Hunt | SaaS, dev tools, consumer apps | 4-6 weeks |
| Email List | Course, info product, SaaS with list | 6-8 weeks |
| Social Media | Consumer product, personal brand | 4-6 weeks |
| Paid Ads | E-commerce, established product | 2-4 weeks |
| Community | Niche product, developer tools | 6-8 weeks |
| Partner | B2B, enterprise, marketplace | 8-12 weeks |
| Hybrid | Any high-stakes launch | 8-12 weeks |

## Step 3: 8-Week Timeline

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation
- [ ] Define positioning: "For [TARGET] who [PROBLEM], [PRODUCT] is a [CATEGORY] that [KEY BENEFIT]. Unlike [ALTERNATIVE], we [DIFFERENTIATOR]."
- [ ] Set up landing page / waitlist
- [ ] Set up analytics and tracking
- [ ] Draft all email sequences
- [ ] Identify 10-20 beta testers
- [ ] Research 20+ communities where audience gathers

### Weeks 3-4: Audience Building
- [ ] Publish 2-3 content pieces related to the problem
- [ ] Share behind-the-scenes content (building in public)
- [ ] Engage in target communities (provide value, don't pitch)
- [ ] Onboard beta testers and collect feedback
- [ ] Begin influencer/partner outreach
- [ ] Set up referral mechanism for waitlist
- [ ] Record demo video or product walkthrough

### Weeks 5-6: Pre-Launch Intensification
- [ ] Send pre-launch email sequence to waitlist
- [ ] Increase social posting to daily
- [ ] Publish case study from beta testers
- [ ] Finalize pricing and offer structure
- [ ] Create launch-day content package (all ready)
- [ ] Brief partners with swipe copy
- [ ] Test all purchase/signup flows end-to-end
- [ ] Set up urgency mechanism (early bird, limited spots)

### Week 7: LAUNCH WEEK

**Monday -- Soft Launch / VIP Access:**
Early access email to VIPs and top waitlist. Collect feedback. Goal: first 50-100 users.

**Tuesday -- Public Announcement:**
Full list email, launch blog post, all social channels, Product Hunt (if applicable), activate partners, begin paid ads.

**Wednesday -- Social Proof Push:**
Share customer testimonials, repost reactions, "look what people are saying" email.

**Thursday -- Objection Handling:**
FAQ post, email addressing top 3 objections, live Q&A, comparison content.

**Friday -- Urgency and Scarcity:**
"Early bird ends soon" email, countdown content, final testimonials.

### Week 8: Post-Launch
- [ ] Send post-launch survey
- [ ] Compile and analyze launch metrics
- [ ] Write launch retrospective
- [ ] Transition to regular pricing
- [ ] Set up onboarding email sequence
- [ ] Follow up with media and partners with results
- [ ] Identify top customers for case studies

## Email Sequences

### Pre-Launch (Weeks 5-6)
1. **The Teaser** (2 weeks before): Build anticipation, hint at product
2. **The Reveal** (1 week before): Show product, share beta results, announce date
3. **The Social Proof** (3 days before): Feature beta testimonials

### Launch Week
4. **The Launch** (Day 1): Announce, state offer clearly, early bird pricing
5. **Social Proof Follow-Up** (Day 3): First customer reactions
6. **Objection Handler** (Day 4): Top 3-5 objections answered
7. **Urgency Close** (Day 5-7): Deadline reminder, recap value

## Social Media Templates

### Twitter/X Thread
```
Post 1: After [time] of building, [Product] is live. It helps [audience] [outcome]. Here's the story:
Post 2: The problem: [detail]
Post 3: The solution: [what product does + screenshot]
Post 4: Early results: [numbers]
Post 5: What's included: [features]
Post 6: Launch offer: [pricing/deal]
Post 7: Try it now: [link]
```

### LinkedIn Post
```
I just launched [Product], and here's why it matters:
[1-2 sentences about the problem]
After [context], I realized [insight].
Early users are seeing: [Result 1] [Result 2] [Result 3]
[Link] -- Special launch pricing for [timeframe].
```

## Partner Coordination

**Timeline:**
- Week 3: Initial outreach
- Week 4: Share product details and demo
- Week 5: Confirm participation, send swipe copy and affiliate links
- Week 6: Reminder with launch schedule
- Week 7: Day-of coordination
- Week 8: Share results, pay commissions

**Provide partners with:**
- Product access
- Swipe copy for email, social, blog
- Branded graphics
- Unique tracking links
- Commission structure
- Launch day schedule

## Launch Metrics

**Track in real-time:**
- Website traffic by source
- Social impressions and engagement
- Email open/click rates
- Signup/purchase conversion rate
- Revenue generated
- Cost per acquisition

**Post-launch retention:**
- Day 1 / Day 7 retention
- Feature adoption rate
- Support ticket volume
- NPS score

## Common Launch Mistakes

1. Launching to nobody -- build audience BEFORE product is ready
2. No urgency mechanism -- without deadline, people forget
3. Perfectionism -- ship at 80%, iterate on feedback
4. Single-channel -- coordinate across email, social, communities
5. No follow-up sequence -- most conversions happen days 3-7
6. No support plan -- launch day generates support requests

## Budget Allocation

| Level | Allocation |
|-------|-----------|
| Bootstrapped ($0-500) | 100% organic: content, communities, email, outreach |
| Moderate ($500-5K) | 40% paid ads, 30% influencer, 20% tools, 10% design |
| Well-Funded ($5K-25K) | 35% ads, 25% influencer, 20% PR, 10% events, 10% tools |
