# Competitive Intelligence Analysis

Identify competitors, analyze their marketing strategies, and produce comprehensive comparison reports with positioning gaps, steal-worthy tactics, and differentiation opportunities.

## When to Use

- Analyzing the competitive landscape for a business
- Building feature and pricing comparison matrices
- Identifying differentiation opportunities
- Creating competitor vs. alternative pages
- Planning competitive positioning strategy

## Phase 1: Competitor Identification

### Categories

| Category | Definition | Count |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Direct | Same product, same audience, same market | 3-5 |
| Indirect | Different product, same problem solved | 2-3 |
| Aspirational | Market leaders the brand aspires to become | 1-2 |

### Discovery Methods

1. **Keyword-Based:** Search primary keywords, "[category] software", "[brand] alternatives", "[brand] vs"
2. **Site-Based:** Check comparison pages, integrations pages, blog mentions
3. **Review Platforms:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot category searches
4. **Community:** Reddit recommendations, Twitter conversations, LinkedIn discussions

## Phase 2: Analysis Framework

### Messaging Analysis

For each competitor capture:
- Exact H1 headline text (reveals positioning)
- Value proposition (core promise)
- Target audience (who they speak to)
- Key differentiator (competitive moat claims)
- Tone of voice (casual/formal/technical)
- Social proof strategy (type and quantity)

### Positioning Map

Plot competitors on two axes:
- X: Perceived simplicity <-> Perceived power
- Y: Perceived affordability <-> Perceived premium

Adjust axes based on what matters most in the specific industry.

### Pricing Comparison

Build detailed matrix with: free plan, starter/pro/enterprise pricing, free trial length, annual discount, per-user vs flat pricing, usage limits.

**Assess:**
- Is target priced above, below, or at market average?
- Is pricing transparent or hidden?
- What pricing model is used?
- Are anchoring tactics being used?

### Feature Comparison

| Feature Category | Feature | Target | Comp A | Comp B |
|-----------------|---------|--------|--------|--------|
| Core | Feature 1 | Full | Full | Partial |
| Advanced | Feature 2 | No | Full | No |
| Integration | Feature 3 | Full | Partial | Full |

Use: Full, Partial, No, or Beta.

Highlight: competitive moats, vulnerability gaps, unique differentiators.

### SEO and Content Analysis

| Metric | Target | Comp A | Comp B |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Blog posts (estimated) | X | X | X |
| Publishing frequency | X/week | X/week | X/week |
| Content depth | Level | Level | Level |

**Content Gap Analysis:** List topics competitors cover that the target does not.

### Social Media Comparison

Compare followers, posting frequency, engagement rate, and top content type across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok.

### Review Mining

For each competitor extract from G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Reddit:
- Overall rating and review count
- Top 3 praised features
- Top 3 complaints
- Common switching reasons
- Most mentioned use cases

## Phase 3: SWOT Analysis

For each competitor and an aggregate for the target brand:
- **Strengths:** Where target outperforms competitors
- **Weaknesses:** Where target lags behind
- **Opportunities:** Gaps no competitor addresses well
- **Threats:** Areas where competitors are significantly stronger

## Phase 4: Strategic Recommendations

### Steal-Worthy Tactics

Identify 5-10 proven marketing tactics from competitors:
- What the tactic is
- Why it works
- How to implement it
- Estimated effort and impact

### Differentiation Strategy

1. **Category:** Can the target create or own a sub-category?
2. **Audience:** Can it own a specific segment competitors ignore?
3. **Feature:** Is there a unique capability no competitor offers?
4. **Philosophy:** Can it differentiate on values or methodology?
5. **Experience:** Can it differentiate on customer experience?

### Alternative Pages Strategy

For each major competitor, outline a "[Target] vs [Competitor]" page:
1. Quick comparison table
2. Where Target wins (3-4 advantages)
3. Where Competitor wins (honest, builds trust)
4. Who Target is best for
5. Customer switching stories
6. Migration guide or switching offer
7. FAQ about switching

### Switching Narratives

For each competitor, develop:
- Primary reasons customers switch
- Switching story template
- Switching incentive (free migration, extended trial, pricing match)

## Phase 5: Ongoing Monitoring

- [ ] Set Google Alerts for competitor names
- [ ] Follow competitors on social media
- [ ] Subscribe to competitor newsletters
- [ ] Check pricing pages monthly
- [ ] Monitor review sites quarterly
- [ ] Track content publishing topics and frequency
- [ ] Watch for product launches and feature updates
- [ ] Monitor job postings (reveals strategic priorities)

### Competitive Response Playbook

| Competitor Move | Response Strategy | Timeline |
|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Price cut | Emphasize value, not price war | 1 week |
| New feature | Assess relevance, communicate roadmap | 2 weeks |
| Aggressive ads | Double down on owned channels, retention | Ongoing |
| Negative comparison | Create factual, balanced comparison | 1 week |
| Major funding | Reassure customers, emphasize focus | 1-2 days |

## Output Structure

Deliver report covering:
1. Executive Summary (landscape, position, top 3 recommendations)
2. Competitor profiles with full analysis
3. Feature, pricing, and review comparison tables
4. Positioning map
5. Content and SEO gap analysis
6. Aggregate SWOT
7. Steal-worthy tactics with implementation guidance
8. Differentiation strategy
9. Alternative page outlines
10. Monitoring plan and response playbook
