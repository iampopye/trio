# Client Proposal Generator

Generate professional, client-ready marketing services proposals that position you as the clear choice, frame pricing strategically, and include ROI projections.

## When to Use

- Creating a proposal for a prospective client
- Formalizing a marketing engagement after discovery
- Building a reusable proposal template for an agency
- Structuring tiered pricing for services

## Step 1: Gather Inputs

**About the Client:**
1. Client name and company
2. Industry and business model
3. Current marketing situation
4. Primary pain points
5. Goals (revenue, growth, leads)
6. Budget range
7. Decision timeline
8. Key stakeholders

**About the Services:**
1. Services proposed (SEO, ads, content, social, email, full-stack)
2. Engagement model (retainer, project, performance-based)
3. Proposed timeline
4. Relevant case studies

## Step 2: Discovery Call Questions

**Business Understanding:**
1. "Walk me through your business model."
2. "Who is your ideal customer?"
3. "What does your sales process look like?"

**Current Marketing:**
4. "What marketing are you doing today? What's working?"
5. "What's your current monthly marketing spend and ROI?"
6. "What tools and platforms are you using?"

**Goals:**
7. "If we're wildly successful, what does that look like in 6 months?"
8. "What specific numbers are you trying to hit?"
9. "What's the lifetime value of a customer?"

**Decision:**
10. "Who else is involved and what's your timeline?"

## Step 3: Proposal Sections

### Cover Page
Company logos, proposal title, prepared for/by, date, valid until date, confidentiality note.

### Executive Summary (1 page max)
- Acknowledge client's situation and goals
- State the core problem you will solve
- Preview your recommended approach
- Hint at expected outcome
- Create urgency

### Situation Analysis (2-3 pages)
1. Current State Overview
2. Opportunities Identified
3. Competitive Landscape
4. Key Challenges
5. Market Context

**Frame everything as opportunities, not failures.**
Good: "Your site converts at 1.8%, below the 3.2% benchmark. We see a clear path to close this gap."
Bad: "Your site has a terrible conversion rate."

### Strategy and Approach (2-3 pages)
- Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2) -- Setup, audits, baselines, quick wins
- Phase 2: Growth (Month 3-4) -- Core execution, optimization
- Phase 3: Scale (Month 5-6) -- Expand winners, cut losers

### Scope of Work (1-2 pages)
Include specific deliverables with quantities, meeting cadence, response time commitments, reporting format.

**Explicitly exclude** items outside scope. List client responsibilities (timely feedback, account access, designated contact).

### Timeline (1 page)
Visual timeline with phases, milestones, and deliverables.

### Investment (1-2 pages)

**Three-Tier Pricing:**

| Component | Growth | Accelerate | Dominate |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Strategy | Quarterly | Monthly | Weekly |
| Content | 4/month | 8/month | 16/month |
| Social | 3 platforms | 5 platforms | All |
| Paid Ads | Up to $5K | Up to $15K | Up to $50K |
| Reporting | Monthly | Bi-weekly | Weekly |
| **Investment** | **$X,XXX** | **$X,XXX** | **$X,XXX** |

**Pricing Psychology:**
- Present 3 options (most choose middle)
- Name tiers aspirationally (not Bronze/Silver/Gold)
- Anchor highest tier first
- Badge middle as "Recommended"
- Show ROI math: "At your LTV, you need X new customers for positive ROI"

### ROI Projection

```
Current State:
  Monthly traffic: [X]
  Conversion rate: [X%]
  Leads/month: [X]
  Average deal value: $[X]

Projected (6 months):
  Traffic increase: [X%]
  Conversion improvement: [X%]
  Revenue increase: $[X]/month
  6-month ROI: [X]x
```

Be conservative. Use ranges. Add disclaimers.

### Team (0.5-1 page)
Name, title, relevant experience, role on the engagement.

### Case Studies (1-2 pages)
2-3 relevant case studies:
- Client: Industry and type
- Challenge: 1-2 sentences
- Solution: 1-2 sentences
- Results: 3 specific metrics

### Next Steps (0.5 page)
1. Sign proposal
2. Kickoff call within 48 hours
3. Onboarding questionnaire
4. Foundation phase begins immediately

## Follow-Up Sequence

- **Day 0:** Send with brief cover note
- **Day 2:** "Making sure you received the proposal"
- **Day 5:** Value-add (article, insight related to their industry)
- **Day 7:** "Would love to hear your thoughts"
- **Day 14:** Final follow-up
- **Day 21:** Graceful breakup email

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as investment, show ROI, offer smaller scope |
| "We can do in-house" | Highlight opportunity cost, expertise, speed |
| "Tried before, didn't work" | Ask what failed, differentiate approach, offer pilot |
| "Need to think" | Set specific follow-up date, address concerns |
| "Can you guarantee results?" | Share historical results, offer performance component |
| "Talking to others" | Welcome it, differentiate on methodology not price |

## Best Practices

- Keep total proposal under 15 pages
- Lead with client's problems, not your services
- Anchor every price to ROI
- Mirror the client's own language from discovery
- Use bold, headers, and tables for scannability
- Always include a specific, time-bound next step
